# 2014

## Year thesis

2014 is the year flat design receives an official physics.

Google announces Material Design at I/O and turns the decade's flatness into a system of paper, depth, elevation, motion, color, typography, components, and rules. Instead of simply removing skeuomorphic texture, Material asks what a digital surface is allowed to do: stack, cast shadow, transform, respond, and guide attention.

At the same time, Apple announces Apple Watch and makes interface design glanceable, bodily, and tiny. Slack launches publicly and shows that workplace software can be colorful, conversational, searchable, and emoji-literate. Airbnb's Belo identity turns a platform logo into a debate about belonging, abstraction, and appropriation. IBM formalizes design language at corporate scale.

The feeling of the year: **flat design becomes an operating discipline**.

2014 is cleaner, more systematic, and more brand-conscious than 2013. The decade's design work is no longer only about making screens flat; it is about maintaining systems across phones, watches, dashboards, clouds, teams, identities, and components.

## How 2014 differs from 2013

2013 makes flat design pop. 2014 gives it rules, components, and institutions.

| From 2013 | To 2014 |
| --- | --- |
| iOS 7 makes flatness bright, thin, and controversial | Material Design gives flatness depth, elevation, motion, and a public specification |
| Slack is in preview | Slack launches publicly and becomes a model for friendly workplace UI |
| Wearable interest is speculative | Apple Watch is announced and glanceable design becomes mainstream anticipation |
| Startup identity is clean and lightweight | Airbnb's Belo shows platform identity can trigger global emotional debate |
| Privacy anxiety darkens platform optimism | Design systems and corporate design languages promise control and trust |
| Mobile UI tools are fragmented | Sketch gains momentum among UI designers as a lighter app-interface tool |

The key shift: 2014 turns interface aesthetics into system governance: patterns, components, motion rules, brand languages, and cross-device behavior.

## Design climate

### The dominant tension

2014 is pulled between **systematic clarity** and **platform intimacy**.

1. **Systematic clarity** - Material Design, IBM Design Language, Android Lollipop, component thinking, Sketch workflows, grids, cards, elevation, and documented motion rules.
2. **Platform intimacy** - Apple Watch on the body, Airbnb's belonging rhetoric, Slack's friendly channels, emoji, notifications, and services that want to feel personal while scaling globally.

The year matters because design systems promise consistency, but the products themselves move closer to bodies, homes, workplaces, and identities. The interface is more governed and more intimate at the same time.

### What is emerging

- **Material as grammar**: cards, elevation, responsive grids, bold color, Roboto, and meaningful motion become a shared Android/web vocabulary.
- **Wearable glances**: tiny screens require hierarchy, haptics, short text, and extreme prioritization.
- **Friendly enterprise software**: Slack makes work tools feel chatty, colorful, searchable, and humane.
- **Platform rebrand backlash**: Airbnb's Belo proves abstract symbols can become cultural flashpoints.
- **Design systems at scale**: IBM's design investment signals that large organizations need shared language, components, and education.
- **Modern UI tooling**: Sketch's popularity reflects a move away from Photoshop-era web mockups toward screen-specific design workflows.
- **Emoji expansion**: Unicode 7.0 adds more emoji and reinforces pictographic expression as interface culture.

## Typography

### The typographic mood

2014 typography becomes system typography.

Material Design's public guidance gives type a governed role: scale, hierarchy, density, baseline grids, button labels, and readable cards. Roboto becomes a visible part of Android's identity. Slack uses readable chat typography where handles, timestamps, channels, emoji, code snippets, and file names all need hierarchy.

The question moves from:

> "How should flat type look?"

to:

> "How should type behave consistently across a product ecosystem?"

### What changes

- **Type scales become documentation**: headings, captions, buttons, and body text are specified as reusable roles.
- **Cards need compact hierarchy**: title, subtitle, action, image, and metadata must work in repeatable modules.
- **Wearables compress language**: glanceable type favors brevity, contrast, and immediate action.
- **Chat creates dense identity type**: handles, channels, bots, emoji, and code fragments mix in one stream.
- **Brand sans neutrality strengthens**: clean geometric and neo-grotesque systems dominate digital products.

## Graphic design

2014 graphic design is the rise of the governed surface.

Material Design turns flat graphics into a rule set: color palettes, icons, cards, shadows, depth, responsive grids, and motion choreography. It is bright but not merely decorative; its argument is that digital material can have consistent behavior.

Brand design also becomes more platform-sensitive. Airbnb's Belo is designed to live as app icon, community symbol, animated mark, signage, and story. Its backlash shows that logos now enter global social interpretation instantly. A symbol is no longer just launched; it is memed, critiqued, remixed, and compared.

## Product and industrial design

2014 product design moves from the hand to the wrist and the room.

Apple Watch is announced as jewelry, sensor, notification surface, fitness device, communication object, and fashion partnership. Its design problem is not only industrial form; it is glance duration, haptics, crown input, complications, bands, and social acceptability.

Amazon Echo points in another direction: ambient voice hardware for the home. Android Wear watches make the wrist a competitive interface category. The product is increasingly a node in a service system: phone, watch, speaker, cloud, notification, account, and identity.

## Architecture and interiors

2014 interiors are shaped by design-system organizations and platform brands.

Corporate design studios, especially IBM's investment in design language and education, make the workplace itself a design instrument: walls for critiques, component libraries, workshops, research rooms, and cross-functional teams. The space supports a repeatable method.

Hospitality and sharing-economy interiors become media assets. Airbnb's promise of belonging depends on photographed homes, host identity, neighborhood imagery, and trust cues. Interiors are no longer only lived spaces; they are listing interfaces.

## Fashion and self-design

2014 fashion is where wearable technology and monochrome minimalism meet.

Apple Watch is announced with fashion-world seriousness: bands, finishes, sizes, gold, sport straps, and wrist presentation. Wearable design must negotiate tech credibility, jewelry, fitness, gender, and etiquette.

At the same time, monochrome hipster/craft identity remains visible: black-and-white logos, tote bags, barber typography, third-wave coffee, minimal sneakers, normcore basics, and carefully unbranded garments. The body is styled for both feed and device.

## Music

2014 music design belongs to streaming scale, surprise releases, and dark minimal identity.

Taylor Swift's *1989* uses Polaroid nostalgia, handwritten marks, and clean pop transformation as a total identity. FKA twigs' *LP1* brings distorted portraiture, flesh, gloss, and digital intimacy into a high-design music world. Aphex Twin's *Syro* uses hyper-detailed packaging and data-like track information as graphic content.

Music identity must survive Spotify rows, Tumblr images, YouTube thumbnails, Instagram posts, vinyl editions, and festival posters.

## Film and moving image

2014 moving image is precise, symmetrical, cosmic, and system-aware.

*The Grand Budapest Hotel* becomes a major reference for color palettes, centered compositions, miniature-like sets, typography, uniforms, packaging, and fictional brand worlds. *Interstellar* uses NASA pragmatism, dust, spacecraft interiors, data displays, and cosmic scale. *The Lego Movie* turns modular bricks into motion language, product world, and comic design system.

Television and streaming design continue rising: title sequences, UI props, and show identities are now watched, clipped, shared, and discussed as design artifacts.

## Color, material, and surface

2014 surfaces are systematic but warm enough to be branded.

Material uses bright color, white cards, shadow, elevation, ripple effects, and paper-like layers. Slack uses aubergine, colorful accents, emoji, and friendly interface density. Airbnb uses coral-red, white, photography, and the Belo mark. Apple Watch uses black glass, stainless steel, aluminum, gold, fluoroelastomer, leather, and Milanese mesh.

Materials: digital paper, card shadows, watch glass, metal bands, haptic taps, app icons, emoji glyphs, chat streams, component libraries, photographed homes, and monochrome craft packaging.

## Flashback design recipes

### Recipe 1: Material card system

Use for: dashboards, Android apps, civic services, education platforms, content products.

- Palette: white, bright blue, teal, amber, red, deep grey.
- Type: Roboto-like sans, documented scale, clear buttons, compact metadata.
- Layout: cards, elevation, responsive grid, floating action button, app bar.
- Imagery: simple icons, color blocks, thumbnails, paper-like panels.
- Motion: ripple, elevation lift, meaningful transition, card expand.
- Risk: making it look like generic flat UI without material behavior.
- Add accuracy with: Google I/O 2014 Material rules and Android Lollipop cues.

### Recipe 2: Glanceable wrist

Use for: health tools, notifications, travel, calendars, lightweight assistants.

- Palette: black glass, white text, activity colors, metallic grey, band color.
- Type: large compact labels, short notifications, high-contrast numerals.
- Layout: tiny hierarchy, complications, single-action screens, circular or modular cues.
- Imagery: icons, rings, contacts, maps, heartbeat, simple illustrations.
- Motion: haptic tap, raise-to-wake, short glance, crown scroll, notification slide.
- Risk: using mature post-2015 Apple Watch UI as if it were already proven.
- Add accuracy with: 2014 announcement-stage anticipation and fashion framing.

### Recipe 3: Friendly work chat

Use for: team software, developer tools, internal systems, support products.

- Palette: aubergine, white, channel grey, green status, emoji brights.
- Type: readable sans, handles, channels, timestamps, code mono.
- Layout: left channel rail, message stream, composer, search, integrations.
- Imagery: avatars, emoji reactions, file previews, bot posts, snippets.
- Motion: notification badge, message arrival, channel switch, upload progress.
- Risk: turning it into a bland enterprise dashboard.
- Add accuracy with: Slack's 2014 public-launch friendliness and density.

### Recipe 4: Belonging platform identity

Use for: marketplaces, travel, community brands, hospitality systems.

- Palette: coral, white, warm grey, photography neutrals, deep charcoal.
- Type: friendly geometric sans, generous spacing, soft brand voice.
- Layout: symbol-first identity, host cards, listing grids, story modules.
- Imagery: homes, hosts, neighborhoods, doors, shared tables, map fragments.
- Motion: logo draw, photo fade, card save, trip flow, trust cue reveal.
- Risk: abstract community language without real trust or specificity.
- Add accuracy with: Airbnb Belo-era debate around belonging and symbol meaning.

### Recipe 5: Monochrome craft interface

Use for: coffee, retail, apparel, small studios, culture brands.

- Palette: black, white, kraft, muted blue, warm grey.
- Type: condensed sans or slab, badge marks, simple web-geometric body.
- Layout: centered logo, product grid, editorial photography, sparse navigation.
- Imagery: hands, tools, packaging, storefronts, process, materials.
- Motion: slow fade, scroll reveal, stamp mark, product hover.
- Risk: generic hipster sameness.
- Add accuracy with: 2014 craft minimalism alongside digital system design.

## Anti-cliches

Do not make 2014 look like:

- iOS 7 only; Material is the year's central system shift.
- Mature design-system tooling from the late 2010s.
- Apple Watch as if users already have years of conventions.
- Airbnb as only a logo controversy with no platform-trust context.
- Slack as a generic chat bubble app.
- Brutalist web or anti-design from later in the decade.
- Corporate Memphis illustration before it dominates.
- Variable fonts before their 2016 announcement.
- Instagram's 2016 gradient logo.

For 2014, the era should feel like **flat design learning rules, wrists, teams, and belonging**.
