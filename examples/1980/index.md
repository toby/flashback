# 1980 example index

1980 is the hinge year: late analog culture starts mutating into postmodern surface, portable media, style magazines, and video-era self-fashioning. Use this index as a fast entry point into the full [1980 corpus](../../corpus/1980.md).

## At a glance

| Theme | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| Postmodern public language | Facades, quotation, double-coding, history as material |
| New Wave and post-punk | Styled instability, broken grids, severe mood, rhythmic layouts |
| Portable media identity | Private sound in public space, objects worn and socially read |
| Pre-digital digitality | Computer-processed imagery, CRT texture, video thinking, analog production |
| Designed self | Fashion, music, editorial, clubs, and identity as one surface |

## Prompt seeds

```text
Design this as if it were made in 1980, at the exact moment when Swiss typography
starts absorbing post-punk, cassette culture, and primitive computer graphics.
Keep it analog, severe, stylish, and slightly unstable.
```

```text
Give me three 1980-informed directions:
1. Post-punk archive
2. New Wave operating manual
3. Portable private world
For each, explain the historical lineage, typography, color, motion, and what to avoid.
```

```text
Critique this interface through a 1980 lens. Where is it too frictionless?
Where could it use stronger materiality, identity, rhythm, or cultural tension?
```

## Useful source artifacts

- Sony TPS-L2 Walkman, cassette tapes, CRT monitors, arcade cabinets, Letraset sheets, paste-up boards.
- *The Face*, Joy Division's *Closer*, Talking Heads' *Remain in Light*, David Bowie's *Scary Monsters (and Super Creeps)*.
- *La Strada Novissima*, Blitz club, the Overlook Hotel, Cloud City, *American Gigolo* interiors.

## Rule of thumb

For 1980, the future should still look assembled by hand.
