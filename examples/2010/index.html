Example recipes

Recipe links into the new 2010 corpus.

Recipe 01

App store confidence

Use for: mobile products, onboarding flows, platform launches.

Palette
white, blue, charcoal, notification red.
Layout
hero phone frames, cards, tab bars, feature stacks.
Motion
tap, slide, refresh, install, confirm.

Risk: generic startup blandness.
Accuracy: keep some 2010 skeuo residue.

 Recipe 02

Social photo feed

Use for: community products, creator tools, visual archives.

Palette
cream, faded black, warm filter tones, app blue.
Layout
image-first cards, comments, likes, stacked posts.
Motion
scroll, like, post, comment, refresh.

Risk: modern creator-platform polish.
Accuracy: keep earlier, simpler social rhythms.

 Recipe 03

Startup map service

Use for: logistics, booking, delivery, travel, mobility.

Palette
white, map green, route blue, graphite.
Layout
split map/card views, buttons, route summaries.
Motion
locate, request, track, confirm, rate.

Risk: drifting into 2016 platform design.
Accuracy: keep visible reassurance and novelty.

 Recipe 04

Tablet living room

Use for: media products, reading tools, home control systems.

Palette
black glass, linen white, warm grey, cyan.
Layout
roomy touch zones, carousels, magazine-like panels.
Motion
swipe, flip, browse, dock, pinch.

Risk: later ultra-flat tablet UI.
Accuracy: keep more chrome and early-touch optimism.

 Recipe 05

Indie-web softness

Use for: editorial products, culture brands, portfolios.

Palette
off-white, washed denim, coral, slate.
Layout
column stacks, large images, quote blocks, soft dividers.
Motion
fade, scroll, hover, expand.

Risk: slipping into 2014 Tumblr maximalism.
Accuracy: stay more restrained and early-social.

Prompt seeds

Three fast prompts for 2010-driven work.

Design this through a 2010 lens: smartphones are the default personal screen, the feed has become the core interface pattern, app ecosystems define brand surfaces, and startup services promise immediate action.
Give me three 2010-informed directions:
1. App store confidence
2. Social photo feed
3. Tablet living room
For each, describe the layout, motion, materials, and what would make it feel too early or too late.
Critique this interface as if it launched in 2010. Is it closer to a social feed, a service app, a tablet reading surface, or an indie-web editorial system?

Anti-cliches

Specificity beats a flattened startup-memory version of the year.

  • Pure flat-design 2015 interfaces
  • Ultra-minimal fintech UI
  • 2007 iPhone launch gloss
  • Social media without timestamps or counters
  • Service apps without maps, ratings, or app-icon logic
